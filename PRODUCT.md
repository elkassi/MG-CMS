# Product

## Register

product

## Users

MG-CMS is used by Lear Trim Tangier teams across CAD, engineering, production, quality, logistics, process, and administration. Users work in an operational manufacturing context where cutting plans, materials, machines, rolls, boxes, and statuses must remain traceable and consistent across MG-CMS, CMS-Prod, scanCoupe, and legacy database mirrors.

## Product Purpose

MG-CMS manages cutting operations from engineering and CAD preparation through logistics release, production execution on the floor, quality follow-up, load planning, and application administration. Success means teams can prepare, import, release, execute, validate, and audit cutting work with reliable data, stable workflows, and clear operational feedback.

The day-to-day operational core is a logistics release flow: a process workbench where teams release sequences, feed cutting tables with the recommended next "best série," and watch the live production floor. Around it sit quality control (cutting and CNC), load planning (plan de charge), chef-de-zone sequence rectification and machine confirmation, and admin tools for system health and data archiving. The earlier scheduling-optimizer screens were retired in favor of this release-driven flow.

## Brand Personality

Precise, practical, production-safe. The interface should feel like a trusted plant tool: clear under time pressure, dense enough for expert users, and restrained enough that workflow state is always more prominent than decoration.

## Anti-references

Do not make MG-CMS feel like a marketing site, decorative demo, consumer dashboard, or experimental UI playground. Avoid heavy visual novelty, gratuitous motion, vague labels, oversized cards, hidden state, and any pattern that makes production data harder to scan or audit.

## Design Principles

Make operational state visible before aesthetic flourish.

Keep filters, tables, actions, and status messages predictable for repeated daily use.

Preserve traceability in the UI: material, plan, placement, machine, project, and status context should stay close to the action.

Use restrained Lear branding and standard controls so expert users can move quickly without relearning the surface.

Show empty, loading, warning, and error states explicitly because production users need to know whether the system is waiting, filtered, blocked, or broken.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use clear contrast, keyboard-friendly controls, visible focus states, and readable table density. Avoid color-only status communication, preserve reduced-motion expectations, and design for plant-floor conditions where screens may be shared, viewed quickly, or operated under ambient light variation.
